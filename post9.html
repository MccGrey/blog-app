MANAGING ANGER

by: Innocent Agu
Jan 15, 2024 || 05:45

Text: Ephesians 4:26-27
26 Be ye angry, and sin not: let not the sun go down upon your wrath:  27 neither give place to the devil.

INTRODUCTION
Anger is a natural phenomenon and so comes naturally when the heart is provoked. We cannot stop it from coming and that is why we are permitted to be angry as seen in our text above. As Christians, we are instructed to exercise self control in all things and that includes over anger. Proverbs 15:8
A wrathful man stirreth up strife: But he that is slow to Anger appeaseth strife.

Proverbs 16:32
He that is slow to Anger is better than the mighty; And he that ruleth his spirit than he that taketh a city.
It is because anger is manageable that we are given additional instructions not to sin when angry or give place to the devil. (Eph. 4:26-27) This means that we have so many options when we are angry: either to obey God’s command on anger or behave like people that don’t have or know God. Whatever we choose to do when angry shows our true identity because not all who profess God knows God.

WHY WE MUST MANAGE ANGER
Anger in the heart of a man is like a volcano in the ground which is extremely hot and gaseous and waiting to erupt. If let out, it devours everything on its way. The difference between volcano and anger is that volcano is natural and so not regulated. In the case of anger, its eruption or otherwise is decided by the man in whose heart it burns. That is why God wants us to manage anger. We therefore manage anger for the following reasons:

Because anger makes us vulnerable to sin We are vulnerable to sin when we are angry. This is because, nothing good comes from anger. All that we do or say out of anger are never in tandem with the spirit of holiness.
James 1:19-20 19
Wherefore, my beloved brethren, let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath:  20 for the wrath of man worketh not the righteousness of God. Those that have conscience usually live to regret most of the things they did or said when they were angry but some without conscience don’t border.

Because a child of God must avoid sin at all cost The problem is that some Christians think that what constitute sin are only such things like fornication or adultery, stealing, lying, worshipping idols, etc. They don’t count malicious words, evil speaking, grudge, etc as sins. Those are such things that usually come from anger. The real children of God must consciously avoid sins that come from anger.

1 John 5:18-20 18
We know that whosoever is born of God sinneth not; but he that is begotten of God keepeth himself, and that wicked one toucheth him not...

EXAMPLES OF POUR ANGER MANAGEMENT
Poor anger management has led some prominent bible characters to grievous mistakes. Some of them are Cain, Moses and Absalom.

Cain
Despite God warning Cain in advance that sin laid on his door, Cain still went ahead to murder his brother Cain because he couldn’t overcome his anger.
Genesis 4:5-7
5 but unto Cain and to his offering he had not respect. And Cain was very wroth, and his countenance fell.  6 And the LORD said unto Cain, Why art thou wroth? and why is thy countenance fallen?  7 If thou doest well, shalt thou not be accepted? and if thou doest not well, sin lieth at the door. And unto thee shall be his desire, and thou shalt rule over him

Moses
Moses was provoked to the core by the children of Israel but there is no justification for sin. Because of anger, he lacked the composure to do exactly as God commanded him and struck the rock instead of speaking to it.
Numbers 20:7-12
7 And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,  8 Take the rod, and gather thou the assembly together, thou, and Aaron thy brother, and speak ye unto the rock before their eyes; and it shall give forth his water, and thou shalt bring forth to them water out of the rock: so thou shalt give the congregation and their beasts drink.  9 And Moses took the rod from before the LORD, as he commanded him. 10 And Moses and Aaron gathered the congregation together before the rock, and he said unto them, Hear now, ye rebels; must we fetch you water out of this rock?  11 And Moses lifted up his hand, and with his rod he smote the rock twice: and the water came out abundantly, and the congregation drank, and their beasts also. 12 And the LORD spake unto Moses and Aaron, Because ye believed me not, to sanctify me in the eyes of the children of Israel, therefore ye shall not bring this congregation into the land which I have given them.

Moses' anger may be justifiable but that is not an excuse for misbehaviour.

Absalom
Absalom harboured anger in his heart against his brother Amnon for a whole two years without saying any bad thing to him until he had killed him for defiling Tamar his sister.

2 Samuel 13:20-29
.... 22 And Absalom spake unto his brother Amnon neither good nor bad: for Absalom hated Amnon, because he had forced his sister Tamar. 23 And it came to pass after two full years, that Absalom had sheepshearers in Baal- hazor, which is beside Ephraim: and Absalom invited all the kings sons....

Contemporary Examples
We have numerous contemporary examples of people who killed their wives like Osinachi’s husband who was accused to have killed his wife. We numerous knowledge of similar events in Nigeria. Wive killing husband, son killing father, friend killing friend, etc.
Even when killing is not involved, other ugly things happen. That is why we shouldn’t allow the sun to go down on our anger as God has commanded but unfortunately, some Christians have carried anger in their hearts against their brethren for longer than Absalom. We may not have physically murdered our brother like Absalom and Cain but we’re not in better status from theirs because Jesus said this:

Matthew 5:21-22
21 Ye have heard that it was said by them of old time, thou shalt not kill; and whosoever shall kill shall be in danger of the judgment:  22 but I say unto you, That whosoever is angry with his brother without a cause shall be in danger of the judgment:

EXAMPLES OF GOOD ANGER MANAGEMENT
We have several examples of good anger management in the bible but I will mention just two.
Esau
Jacob took away Esau’s blessings as the firstborn but when he came back with his family, servants and great wealth to Mamre. He feared the worst from Esau but Esau’s action showed good management of anger.
Genesis 33:3-4
3 And he passed over before them, and bowed himself to the ground seven times, until he came near to his brother.  4 And Esau ran to meet him, and embraced him, and fell on his neck, and kissed him: and they wept.

Joseph
Joseph had opportunity to take his own pound of flesh from his brothers who plotted to kill him but later sold him to Ishmaelites. When they mourned for their error on realising that Joseph is now a governor in Egypt and their host, Joseph shocked them with words of consolation and comfort.

Genesis 45:4-5
4 And Joseph said unto his brethren, Come near to me, I pray you. And they came near. And he said, I am Joseph your brother, whom ye sold into Egypt.  5 Now therefore be not grieved, nor angry with yourselves, that ye sold me hither: for God did send me before you to preserve life.

HOW TO CONTROL ANGER
It is simple to control anger. There are two easiest steps to control anger.
The first step is to say nothing when you are angry

Psalm 39:1-2
1 I said, I will take heed to my ways, That I sin not with my tongue: I will keep my mouth with a bridle, While the wicked is before me. 2 I was dumb with silence, I held my peace, even from good; And my sorrow was stirred.

The second step is not to prolong the anger.
Ephesians 4:26-27
26 Be ye angry, and sin not: let not the sun go down upon your wrath:  27 neither give place to the devil.

CONCLUSION

As Christians, we should have the ability to bring our emotions under control.

Proverbs 19:11
The discretion of a man deferreth his anger; And it is his glory to pass over a transgression. When a Christian applies discretion in his emotions, it helps him to avoid sins that usually come with anger.

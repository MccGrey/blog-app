THE UNHOLY UNION - THE INFLUENCE OF CONSTANTINE

by: Innocent Agu
Feb 1st, 2024 || 10:45

INTRODUCTION

In the book of Revelations 18:2,4 we read: “…Babylon the great is fallen, is fallen, and is become the habitation of devils, and the hold of every foul spirit, and a cage of every unclean and hateful bird.” …4  And I heard another voice from heaven, saying, Come out of her, my people, that ye be not partakers of her sins, and that ye receive not of her plagues.” This means that this symbolic Babylon was once pure and glorious but suddenly changed to something else. This is a vision about the glorious church of God that was corrupted by the merger with the pagan Roman Empire which removed the global oversight of the Church from Christ to the Roman Empire through its emperors and popes. Instead of the church being subject to Christ according to Ephesians 5:24, it became subject to the religious decrees of the Roman Emperors and Roman Pontiffs. By this shift, the Church ultimately seized from relying wholly on the laws of Christ through the Bible but began to rely on the Decrees of the Roman Emperors and Pontiffs. What it means is that the Church became the Church of Rome rather than the Church of God.

It also resulted in the church having a set of doctrines that is a mixture of Bible and Roman traditions. At a stage, Popes could install and dispose kings. At a point, there were no more emperors; Pope became the overall political and religious ruler of the entire world and made bishops temporal (material or political) Lords over regions from which they got the title: “His Lordship”. This assumption of a mixture of political and spiritual duties by the church turned the Church of God into a government cum spiritual organization of the Roman Empire. By this, the fall of the symbolic Babylon was completed.

Today, we are going to look at how Emperor Constantine midwifed this unholy union between the Church of God and the Roman Empire.


THE WAR OF MILVIAN BRIDGE IN 312AD AND OF THE CHRYSOPOLIS IN 324AD

After the defeat of Maxentius, Constantine then decided to unify the entire empire under his authority. On his way to Rome to effect the overthrow of his opposition, he was said to have seen a sign of the cross in the sky with the Greek inscription “By this conquer.”  In 323, Constantine defeated Valerius Licinius who had been a claimant to the throne and a persecutor of Christians and with whom he previously co-authored the Edict of Milan. Following this victory, he became the sole Emperor of the entire Roman Empire.

According to Constantine’s biographer Eusebius, in the night of the incidence of the cross of light in the sky with the Greek inscription, “By this conquer”, Constantine had a dream in which Christ allegedly reinforced the message. The emperor marked the symbol of the cross on his soldiers’ shields. When he triumphed over Licinius at the battle of Chrysopolis in 324AD, he attributed the victory to the God of the Christians although there is no evidence of this recognition in the inscriptions and carvings of his Victory Arch in Rome. Modern scholars still debate the tale and whether Constantine’s conversion was sincere or a political maneuver. Regardless, in 313AD Constantine had met with Licinius, the Eastern Emperor, and together they issued the Edict of Milan. The edict granted “to the Christians and other religions full authority to observe that religion which each preferred.” This Edict was not only for Christians but for all religions within the Roman Empire.


HOW CONSTANTINE TACTICALLY INFLUENCED CHRISTIANITY

Following his claim of defeating Valerius Licinius through the help of the God of the Christians and previously issuance of the Edict of Milan, he bought the hearts of the global Christian community who saw in him a Saviour sent to deliver them from the fiery persecutions.

There was no moral aspect of Constantine’s life that suggested that he was truly a Christian convert because he maintained devotion to the sun god. Throughout his reign, he paid allegiance to the sun god and honoured the sun god in many ways. This shows that he probably embraced Christianity just to ensure the social stability, unity and peace of his reign over the Empire than personal religious convictions or love for the Christian God. He never really accepted to be baptized until his death when he was sprinkled as baptism on his death bed.

The historian says that “In order to preserve the ‘UNITY’ of the church, Constantine took a hand in all internal affairs.” Mattox, F.W. (1961, pg 127) ‘The Eternal Kingdom’. In page 132 of the same book, we have this extract: “This active interest in behalf of the church led Christians to exalt Constantine and rely on his decisions. He soon took the affairs of the church into his own hands and became dictatorial.” Now, a devout believer and worshipper of Sol-Invictus (Unconquered Sun) is the overall controller of the Church of God. How could once glorious Babylon not have become a den of demons? (Rev. 18:1-4)

God couldn’t have considered Constantine a Christian since he maintained devotion to the sun god and managed the Church unbaptized at the same time. It was a case of an idol worshipper high-jacking the management of the church.

“Ye cannot drink the cup of the Lord, and the cup of devils: ye cannot be partakers of the Lord's table, and of the table of devils.” (1 Corinthians 10:21)










CONSTANTINE’S RESPECT AND HONOUR FOR THE DAY OF THE SUN AND NOT FOR CHRISTIANITY IS SHOWN IN THE WORDING OF HIS EDICT FOR WORK-FREE DAY ON SUN’S DAY

Constantine decreed (March 7, 321) dies Solis—day of the sun, “Sun-day”—as the Roman day of rest [CJ3.12.2]:

“On the venerable day of the Sun let the magistrates and people residing in cities rest, and let all workshops be closed. In the country however persons engaged in agriculture may freely and lawfully continue their pursuits because it often happens that another day is not suitable for grain-sowing or vine planting; lest by neglecting the proper moment for such operations the bounty of heaven should be lost.”

There is not a mention or reference to Christianity as the reason for the Edict but to venerate the day of Sun. The phrase the venerable day of the Sun shows his allegiance, respect and inner feelings. He proudly dedicated that day in HONOUR of the Sun and not to allow Christians to worship without distractions because he didn’t say so nor showed any sign for such unwarranted conclusion.


STRONG CLAIM THAT NATALIS INVICTI OF DEC. 25 WAS NOT CELEBRATED BEFORE 4TH CENTURY AD. A SUGGESTION IT WAS CONSTANTINE’S CREATION

Sol Invictus and Christianity
The Philocalian calendar of 354 CE gives a festival of "Natalis Invicti" on 25 December. There is limited evidence that this festival was celebrated before the mid 4th century CE. If it was not, then Constantine probably inspired it.

Whether the 'Sol Invictus' festival "has a strong claim or the responsibility for the December 25 date" of Christmas as per the 1908 Catholic Encyclopedia or not has been called into question by Joseph Cardinal Ratzinger, later Pope Benedict XVI, who challenged this theory by arguing that a December 25 date was determined simply by calculating nine months beyond March 25, regarded as the day of Jesus’ conception (the Feast of the Annunciation). In the 5th century, Pope Leo I (the Great) spoke of how the celebration of Christ's birth coincided with the sun's position increasing in the sky in several sermons on the Feast of the Nativity. Here is an excerpt from his twenty-sixth sermon: “But this Nativity which is to be adored in heaven and on earth is suggested to us by no day more than this when, with the early light still shedding its rays on nature, there is borne in upon our senses the brightness of this wondrous mystery.”

Comment: By this, he is trying to justify the connection of Christmas with the Sun which evidently shows that Christmas is a mere Christianization of Natalis Invicti. It is evident that Christianity has nothing to do with December 25 celebration but the feast being celebrated in disguise is Natalis Invicti. The above Popes’ arguments have not successfully separated Christmas from Natalis Invicti. There are too many Roman Catholic lies.

CONSTANTINE’S INFLUENCE ON THE ORGANIZATION OF THE CHURCH

God designed the Church of God to be autonomous such that each congregation should have multiple resident bishops and multiple resident Deacons and an Evangelist or Evangelists. There is no cluster system or grouped or centralized administrative structure or system.

Paul and Timotheus, the servants of Jesus Christ, to all the saints in Christ Jesus which are at Philippi, with the bishops and deacons. (Philippians 1:1)

 And when they had ordained them elders in every church, and had prayed with fasting, they commended them to the Lord, on whom they believed. (Acts 14:23)

In order to have control over the church, Constantine ensured that Christian bishops were grouped and structured according to the Empire’s administrative system such that some bishops will be under other bishops, thereby changing the law of God. The Council of Nicaea in 325AD exalted three bishops (Antioch, Alexandria and Rome) above the rest who they called Metropolitans and subjected other bishops in their various regions under them thereby ensuring organizational departure of the Church from Scriptural design. The number of the Metropolitans was later increased to four following the building of the city of Constantinople and the elevation of its bishop to Metropolitan. This restructuring of the administrative framework of the church later crystalized in overall one man rule called Pope, hence fulfilling the prophecy of Paul about the Man of Sin. (2 Thessalonians 2:3-4)

Constantine calling himself Bishop of Bishops chaired over the Council of Nicaea which he convened in 325AD that created the first creed of the church which himself drafted and edited after the resolutions of about 318 bishops in attendance. The Bible is complete enough to be the only book of religious authority. No further creed is needed.

16  All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness: 17  That the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly furnished unto all good works. (2 Timothy 3:16-17 )

Christian teaching about God had always pointed out to one Deity called God or The Father whose word was made flesh and was born as a man and called Jesus and whose Spirit was called The Holy Spirit; who in his fullness (with his Word and Spirit) is called the Godhead as a single Deity .

One God and Father of all, who is above all, and through all, and in you all. (Ephesians 4:6 )

Constantine himself later received communion from an Arian priest and, so, he truly did not believe in the eternal Jesus but in the created Jesus meanwhile, Bible shows that Jesus, as Word had always existed with God and share the same eternal beginning with Him for God never at any time, existed without his Word.

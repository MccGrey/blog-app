WHY THE TERM DENOMINATION IS OFFENSIVE TO THE CHRISTIAN RELIGION

by: Innocent Agu
Jan 22, 2024 || 10:45
INTRODUCTION

A lot of professing Christians feel free to boldly say, I belong to this denomination or I belong to that denomination without knowing that they are indicting themselves before God just because, they seem not to understand the meaning of the term “DENOMINATION” and commandment of God against it. The word Denomination does not resonate with God because it counters the principles of Christianity as designed by God. In this lesson, we are going to see why the term “DENOMINATION” is offensive to the Christian religion.


WHAT IS THE MEANING OF THE TERM DENOMINATION?

Advanced English Dictionary defines Denomination in three different ways:


First Definition:

A group of religious congregations having its own organization and distinct faith.


Why is this offensive?

What the Bible says: Bible says we should not be distinct from each other or have distinct faith but that all Christians should have one faith and speak the same thing and be of one mind.

1 Corinthians 1:10:
Now I beseech you, brethren, by the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, that ye all speak the same thing,

and that there be no divisions among you; but that ye be perfectly joined together in the same mind and in the same judgment.  Ephesians 4:5: 5 one Lord, one faith, one baptism.

Philippians 1:27
Only let your conversation be as it becometh the gospel of Christ: that whether I come and see you, or else be absent, I may hear of your affairs, that ye stand fast in one spirit, with one mind striving together for the faith of the gospel; 

1 Timothy 1:3:
3 As I besought thee to abide still at Ephesus, when I went into Macedonia, that thou mightest charge some that they teach no other doctrine,

Second Definition:

Identifying word or words by which someone or something is called and classified or distinguished from others.



Why is this offensive?

What the Bible says: Bible says that there should not be variance or distinctions among us neither should we separate from the original one group or church. It also forbids us from going beyond what is written by assuming new names.

Galatians 5:19-21:
19 Now the works of the flesh are manifest, which are these; Adultery, fornication, uncleanness, lasciviousness,  20 idolatry, witchcraft, hatred, variance, emulations, wrath, strife, seditions, heresies,  ... and such like: of the which I tell you before, as I have also told you in time past, that they which do such things shall not inherit the kingdom of God.

1 John 2:19:
They went out from us, but they were not of us; for if they had been of us, they would no doubt have continued with us: but they went out, that they might be made manifest that they were not all of us.



Third definition:

A class of one kind in a system.



Why is this offensive?

What the Bible says: Bible says that we should not differ from one another because by one Spirit, we are baptized into only one body. Again, Jesus prayed that all his disciples should be made perfect in one so the idea of “one kind in a system” is a rebellion to Jesus wish that all believers should be perfectly one.

John 17:21-23
21 that they all may be one; as thou, Father, art in me, and I in thee, that they also may be one in us: that the world may believe that thou hast sent me.  22 And the glory which thou gavest me I have given them; that they may be one, even as we are one:  23 I in them, and thou in me, that they may be made perfect in one; and that the world may know that thou hast sent me, and hast loved them, as thou hast loved me.

1 Corinthians 4:6-7: 6 And these things, brethren, I have in a figure transferred to myself and to Apollos for your sakes; that ye might learn in us not to think of men above that which is written... 7 For who maketh thee to differ from another?

THE GOD-FRIENDLY HEART

by: Innocent Agu
Jan 27, 2024 || 09:45

Deuteronomy 4:4
But ye that did cleave unto the LORD your God are alive every one of you this day

A God-friendly heart is a heart that is very fond of God, loves God, fears God, anchors/holds on to God, trusts God, believes God, makes sacrifices for God, humbles himself before God, defends the name of God and hopes on God. This is the quality of hearts God wants to live in heaven for all eternity.


INTRODUCTION

In 2 Chronicles 16:9,
we read, “For the eyes of the LORD run to and fro throughout the whole earth, to shew himself strong in the behalf of them whose heart is perfect toward him. ”

This message is about the disposition of our hearts towards God which ultimately reflects in how we live or behave in and outside the church. How we talk in and outside the church, how we reason in and outside the church, how we relate with others in and outside the church, etc.

We are going to briefly look at the lives of some Bible characters that loved God and draw inspiration from them.


SOME BIBLE CHARACTERS WITH GOD-FRIENDLY HEARTS

The list will be much so let’s take a few:

ABEL: - He owed nobody anything but when he and his brother Cain were to make sacrifices to God, he conditioned his heart to think and believe that God deserved the best of all he had. (Gen. 4:4) Do you give to God freely or joyfully or only when you are pushed?

ENOCH: - His name is defined as “Dedicated” and he lived in the days when there was no law or human government . People lived wildly as they liked but Enoch carved out a lifestyle for himself that was anchored on the fear of God so much that God did not even allow him to die. (Gen. 5:22-24) Do you like to enjoy your right at all times or do you sacrifice your freedom just for righteousness to rule?

NOAH: - Like Enoch, Noah had every opportunity to enjoy his freedom to live as he liked because in his days, evil was so much that God cursed the land (Gen. 5:29), but he chose to be different and to direct his household towards the fear of God. (Gen. 6:9) What do you do in the midst of ungodly men? Do you join in their worldly jokes or promote Christ in their midst?

ABRAHAM: - He came from an idol worshipping region of Chaldean. Remember that Laban was Abraham’s step brother and brother to Abraham’s wife – Sarah but he had idols in his house one of which Rachel stole when Jacob’s family were leaving. (Gen. 31:30-32). Coming from such background, Abraham maintained a righteous lifestyle that made God to call him and asked him to leave that region and afterwards called him “My friend”. (Gen. 12:1, Isaiah 41:8) What do you do when people push for chaos in and outside the church? Do you add fire instead of being the lone righteous voice?

JACOB: - Barring his craftiness in stealing in stealing the blessings of Esau which he legitimately purchased from Esau with the porridge earlier, Jacob showed his faithfulness and trustworthiness through his service to Laban for twenty years. (Gen. 31:36-42). How trustworthy have you been in the things or works of God. Do you disappear when work or evangelism is organized?

JOSEPH: - Joseph had already left his father’s land where moral values were upheld and promoted. In Egypt, nobody was teaching him or rebuking him on moral issues just like some children that left their families to join life in the campus but yet, because Joseph had conditioned his heart to be God-friendly, he accepted to suffer than to sleep with his master’s wife. (Gen. 39:7-9) How do you live out there outside the church environment? Do you play double standard or maintain purity even in secret places?

PHINEHAS: - A young man son of Eleazar the priest saw a young Hebrew man brought a Moabite lady into the camp during the Exodus. All the elders including Moses saw him and only lamented but didn’t take any action. Because Phinehas had already conditioned his heart to defend the will of God went after the man and with his spear pinned the man and the woman to the ground. (Num. 25:6-8). What do you do when someone or some people are bringing the church or the name of God to disrepute? Do you watch from the the sidelines or take action?

DAVID: - Even though he was not a trained soldier, but because his heart had been conditioned not to hear and accept insult on the name of God, he risked his life to redeem the honour of God even when the captain’s of Israeli soldiers and the king himself showed no concern. God earlier called him the man after my heart foreseeing the kind heart David had towards him. (1 Sam. 17:26, 1 Samuel 13:14) Have you risked getting enemies for yourself just because you are fighting for righteousness?

SHEDRACK, MESHACH AND ABADNEGGO: - They had a reasonable excuse to worship an idol because it was a forceful order from the overall king of the Empire. Despite that failure to oblige was death, they had conditioned their hearts never to compromise their religious convictions; not even in a bid to obey the government. (Daniel 3:16-18). Do you compromise your Christian values because your boss in the office compelled you to?

STEPHEN: - In his preaching to the Jews, he still told them the truth not considering the hostility that would come from them. (Acts 6 & 7). Do you always tell people the truth when need be or do you prefer to keep quiet to avoid offending or having problems with anyone?


CONCLUSION

We can make ourselves look beautiful before God. Not by beauty of our fabrics but by the beauty of lives, relationships, mentality, devotion, commitment, sacrifice and general godliness.

Peter admonished the Christian women that their beauty should not be outward adorning but the hidden man of the heart. (1 Peter 3:3-4). This admonition goes to all Christians. We must nurse our hearts to God-friendly.
